What is Li-Fi?

Li-Fi (Light Fidelity) is a bidirectional, high-speed, and fully networked wireless communication technology using visible light, UV, and infrared spectrum. Coined by Prof. Harald Haas in 2011.

⚡

Advantages

  • ◈ 100× faster than Wi-Fi

01

LED Transmitter

High-brightness LED array modulated at frequencies from 1 kHz to 100 MHz. Imperceptible to human eye — visible light carries encoded binary data streams.

Wavelength: 450–700nm · Power: 1–10W
02

Driver Circuit

PWM-based LED driver with precision current control. Handles OOK (On-Off Keying) and OFDM modulation schemes for reliable high-speed encoding.

Freq: up to 100MHz · Modulation: OOK/OFDM
03

Optical Channel

Free-space visible light propagation medium. Line-of-sight or diffuse transmission with multipath support in reflective environments.

Range: 1–10m · Band: 400–700nm
04

Photodiode Receiver

Silicon PIN photodiode with avalanche gain option. Converts incident photons to electrical current with high sensitivity and fast response time.

Responsivity: 0.5 A/W · BW: >200MHz
05

Microcontroller

Central processing unit managing both TX encoding and RX decoding. Handles framing, error correction (CRC), buffering, and protocol stack execution.

MCU: ESP32 @ 240MHz · RAM: 520KB
06

Display Module

OLED/LCD display for real-time decoded message output and system diagnostics. Shows received data, BER metrics, and signal quality indicators.

OLED: 128×64px · Interface: I2C/SPI
